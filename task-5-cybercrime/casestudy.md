## Description of the Crime:
Cyberbullying via identity theft and stalking involves a perpetrator creating fake or multiple social media profiles to harass, monitor, or annoy a target. This is often done to bypass "blocks" placed by the victim on their primary accounts.

## Step-by-Step Scenario:
*Creation:* The perpetrator sets up multiple "sock-puppet" accounts using fake names or stolen photos to appear as a different person or remain anonymous.
*Stalking:* They use these profiles to follow the victim’s public activities, monitor their locations, and identify their circle of friends.
*Harassment:* The perpetrator begins sending persistent, annoying, or abusive messages. If the victim blocks one account, the harasser immediately switches to another fake profile to continue the abuse.
*Identity Faking:* In some cases, the harasser may impersonate a mutual friend or a faculty member to gain sensitive information from the victim.
## Targets and Consequences:
While anyone can be targeted, college students are often high-risk due to their high digital activity. The consequences are severe, ranging from extreme mental distress and anxiety to academic decline and long-term reputational damage.
